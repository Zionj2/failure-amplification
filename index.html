A short, structured guide

Failure amplification is when a small failure doesn’t stay small.

Many systems don’t collapse because of one dramatic mistake. They collapse because small, manageable issues interact, spread, and compound—until recovery becomes harder than the original fix.

Working definition

Failure amplification is the process where initially manageable problems grow in impact because of feedback loops, hidden coupling, and delayed detection.

Core amplification drivers

Delay hides damage

If consequences arrive late, you keep doing the thing that caused them. By the time the signal appears, the system has already accumulated debt.

Coupling spreads impact

Tightly connected parts share failure. One component running “a little hot” becomes everyone’s problem when there’s no slack.

Workarounds create new risk

Workarounds can be rational in the moment—but they often move risk to places you aren’t watching, and make the next failure more likely.

Feedback loops accelerate

Once pressure creates behaviors that increase pressure, the system enters a loop. Loops are where “small” becomes “runaway.”
