Scenario

“It’s probably fine” → Too late

When alerts are noisy, response delay becomes the amplifier.

Patterns involved

Alert Fatigue Delay Workaround Debt

Reference: Alert Fatigue

The cascade

  1. 1

    Alerts fire often

    The system is noisy: lots of warnings, many false alarms, unclear severity.

    Amplifier seed: signal distrust.

  2. 2

    People learn to ignore the signal

    On-call has been burned by noise. The default response becomes “wait and see.”

    Amplifier: human delay.

  3. 3

    Workarounds accumulate

    Instead of fixing root causes, small mitigations pile up: thresholds bumped, timeouts increased.

    Amplifier: workaround debt (fragility grows quietly).

  4. 4

    A real incident looks “normal” at first

    The early warning signs blend into background noise. Response is late and less effective.

    Amplifier: late intervention (less leverage).

  5. 5

    Recovery cost spikes

    By the time action happens, queues/backlogs have formed and collateral damage spreads.

    Key idea: early action is cheap; late action is expensive.

How to interrupt this cascade

Reduce noise

Delete or downgrade low-signal alerts. Make severity unambiguous.

Practice response

Run drills so real signals trigger confident action, not hesitation.

Track workaround debt

Give workarounds expiry dates and owners so temporary fixes don’t become permanent fragility.
