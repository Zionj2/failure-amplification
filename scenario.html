Featured Scenario

The “minor slowdown” that becomes a full outage

Nothing explodes at first. The system degrades, people compensate, compensations add load, and the loop accelerates.

Patterns involved

Retry Storm Queue Collapse Hidden Coupling Alert Fatigue No Slack

These patterns are defined in Patterns.

The cascade

  1. 1

    Small change increases latency

    A feature adds an inefficient database query. Requests still succeed, but average response time rises. It’s noticeable, not alarming.

    Amplifier: delay (damage accumulates quietly).

  2. 2

    Time-outs begin during busy moments

    A small fraction of requests time out. Users refresh. Refreshes add traffic. The problem feels “intermittent,” so it’s easy to downplay.

    Amplifier: feedback loop (retries create load).

  3. 3

    Retries and queues hide severity

    Retry logic and queues smooth over failures. Metrics show “success,” but the system now spends more work to achieve the same results.

    Amplifier: masking (success rate hides cost).

  4. 4

    A workaround makes things worse

    Someone increases timeouts or disables a safeguard so the app feels better briefly. Requests sit longer, holding resources and increasing contention.

    Amplifier: workarounds (risk moves and grows).

  5. 5

    Coupling spreads the pain

    API instances pile up waiting on the database. Autoscaling adds instances, which add more DB connections, which slows the DB further.

    Amplifier: coupling (scaling becomes load).

  6. 6

    Alert fatigue delays response

    Alerts have fired before for “minor” spikes. The team hesitates. Meanwhile the loop continues and the system runs near its limit with no slack.

    Amplifier: human delay (signal ignored until late).

  7. 7

    Runaway failure

    Latency increases → retries increase → load increases → latency increases. Queues fill, timeouts surge, and the database starts rejecting connections. The app appears down.

    Amplifier: positive feedback (self-accelerating).

  8. 8

    Recovery is harder than prevention

    Even after fixing the query, the system must drain backlog, unwind retries, and restore stable behavior. The “small” failure demanded “big” recovery work.

    Amplifier: inertia (unwinding takes time).

Takeaways

Watch cost, not just success

A system can be “mostly successful” while becoming dramatically more expensive per request. Rising work-per-result is an early warning.

Retries are a hidden amplifier

Retries improve experience until they become the main source of load. Make retries visible and bounded.

Slack prevents cascades

Time margin, capacity margin, and attention margin matter. Slack isn’t waste; it’s stability.
